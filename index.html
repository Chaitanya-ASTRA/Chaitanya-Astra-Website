Astra's Event Spectra

Guest Lecture on Indian Space Exploration Saga

Dr. Jaya Saxena, a renowned scientist from the National Remote Sensing Centre (NRSC) in Hyderabad, delivered an insightful guest lecture on the captivating saga of Indian space exploration, highlighting the significant milestones, technological advancements, and future aspirations that define India's journey in space research and satellite technology.

Guest Lecture on the Indian Space Ecosystem

Mr. Radha Krishna Kavuluru, Principal Engineer at Dhruva Space, delivered an engaging guest lecture providing an in-depth overview of the Indian space ecosystem. The lecture covered the orgin and history of rockets in various countries across the world and emphasized on how partnerships between governament and private space agencies are shaping India's dynamic space ecosystem for future advancements.

Workshop on Orbital Mechanics and Mission Planning.

Astra's Workshop on Orbital Mechanics and Mission Planning was an engaging session led by our President Vikranth and Vice President Sreshta. The workshop covered key topics like Orbital Planning, Mission Planning, Trajectory Design and Types of Orbits giving participants a glimpse into the intricacies of space exploration. It was an interactive and insightful experience for all the participants who have attended the workshop.

Guest Lecture on remote sensing for defense applications

Astra hosted a Guest Lecture on Remote Sensing for Defense Applications by Smt. Hari Priya Sakethapuram, a Scientist/Engineer at the National Remote Sensing Centre, ISRO. She explained what remote sensing technology is and what are its applications and advancements in Defence Sector, her expertise and experience in ISRO offered a deep understanding of how remote sensing contributes to national security and defense strategies. The lecture was informative and interactive and provided a valuable learning experience for all attendees.

NISAR: A Collaborative Earth Observation Satellite by NASA and ISRO

NISAR (NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar) is a groundbreaking Earth observation satellite, set to launch in 2024, developed jointly by ISRO and NASA.
